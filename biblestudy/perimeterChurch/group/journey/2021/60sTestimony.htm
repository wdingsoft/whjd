Dear Herschel,
 
Thank you for your important reminder. Here is my reflection and experience to share within class. Please correct me for any kind of error or improper expression. 
 
Before I came to America, I had never heard of Easter day in China. After I came to America and before I was a Christian, I thought of Easter day as an egg hunting festival.  It was after I became a Christian, I gradually knew the meaning of Easter day deeper and deeper. Your email makes me realize that Easter Day is the most important day among all holidays in the world. It is even more important than the most popular Christmas day when Jesus was born. Chrstmas day reminds us a good news (the Gospel) -- the Son of God came into the world and He showed us the way and truth, so that we did not need to bitterly explore or struggle for our ideal society by ourselves, and we can live with peace and love by following Him in the world. Whereas Easter day reminds us that Jesus won -- He had defeated all kinds of evils and gods, all kinds of crafty enemies, suppressors, all kinds of addictions and sins, and all kinds of powers of injustice both in air and on earth. Moreover, He defeated the most fearful thing for all of us that is death. 
 
Everyone wants to win. I used to explore Chinese Daoism, Confucius and all kinds of Chinese philosophies to get wisdom and soft power; I also used to explore Buddhism in order to get rid of the bitterness of life. But the deeper I dug into it, the more pessimistic I became. Right after 8964, a friend gave me a comfortable Buddhism wisdom: "The sadness is not better than the death of your soul." Essentially, it infers a soul suicide. 
 
I have to give thanks to the church and all Christians who ever helped me. Most importantly, I have to give thanks to the Lord Jesus Christ. He forgave all my sins and removed all my evil addictions.  I was baptized in 2003. After I turned to Him, the more I read the Bible, the more I enjoyed life. "He restores my soul. He guides me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake." (Psm23:3 NIV) Because He was crucified on the Cross, He took over all my pain and sadness. Because He won, so I became a winner and all believers are the winners. Easter Day is the only true victory day that is worth celebrating in the world.
